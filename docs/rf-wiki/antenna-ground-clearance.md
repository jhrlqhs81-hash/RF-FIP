Antenna ground clearance and keepout problems should be considered when OTA TIS or EIS changes with nearby metal, bracket, cover, tape, screw, or ground contact conditions. Compare assembled and open states, inspect clearance to conductive structures, and separate antenna radiation effects from conducted RF chain performance.

The next action should identify the changed structure, its distance or contact state, and whether the fail follows antenna path loading rather than receiver chain degradation.
